The Latest Tech for Collecting Personal Data

In this digital age, smartphones has now became an essential tool for many people because of its convenience. Developers are constantly designing apps to help people with their everyday tasks and increase efficiency. There is no doubt about the growing demand for better designed apps with a variety of functionalities. However, many of these phone applications require user information in order to carry out certain tasks. The large amount of personal information collected could be broadcasted or even sold to third-party sources. This could possibly compromise the user’s privacy and personal data.

Data Sharing Risks

Full Network Access

This allows the app to have access to the internet. If the function of your app doesn’t require communication with the outside world, chances are this permission is meant for ads. However full network access could also mean software updates, syncing or retrieving data online.

Modify / Delete Storage

As suggested by the description, it gives the app permission to save, edit, or delete files on your phone. Most apps use this to store temporary files and logs, but in theory it has access to all of your documents, including your SD card, so be aware!

Phone Status and Identity

This permission allows the app to recognize when a phone call is coming and when it is active. It can also access more of the sensitive information such as your phone number and your device’s IMEI number.

Location

This feature is used mostly by apps that wants to display relative content depending on your location. At the same time, it could also just be the implementation of location-based advertising.

What can you do about it?

It is inevitable that some of your information will be needed for the functionality of the app. After all, an app won’t be able to tell you the nearest coffee shop without knowing your location. The best defence is to always stay alert and be aware of what apps are asking from you. Don't just click “yes” to everything, but actually look at the permissions on what you’ve agreed to. If in doubt, don’t downloading the app. compromising your personal data for convenience or pleasure is just isn’t worth it.
